    üìä Methodology & Data Accuracy

    Q: How did you calculate the 10-12% productivity gain?

    A: We use person-week averaging methodology. For each individual each week, we calculate (hours saved √∑ 40 hours) √ó 100, then average across all individuals and weeks. This accounts for varying team sizes and provides the most accurate measurement based on 2,243 person-weeks of real data across 7 SDLC phases.

    Q: How confident are you in these numbers?

    A: Very confident. We have 87.9% data quality with 3,602 verified tasks across 563 team members. The main gap is 422 anonymized entries (likely bulk submissions). All calculations are validated against source data and follow consistent methodology throughout.

    Q: Why does the graph show a "wave" pattern instead of steady growth?

    A: The waves reflect data collection timing, not actual usage. Peak weeks (150-210 people, 900-1,100 hrs) align with sprint ends when teams document AI usage. Valley weeks show minimal activity. True AI adoption is likely higher than reported. We're working to improve consistent reporting from 10% to 40% weekly participation.

    How is Overall % Productivity Gain Calculated?

    New Enhanced Methodology:

    We use a person-week averaging method that provides more accurate productivity measurements by accounting for individual contributions.

    üìä Calculation Steps:

    1. Group by Individual: For each week, we group tasks by person and phase
    2. Calculate Per-Person Gain: Hours Saved √∑ 40 hours/week √ó 100
    3. Average Within Week: Average all individuals' gains for that week
    4. Average Across Weeks: Average all weekly gains to get phase average
    5. Range Buffer: Apply ¬±15% to show expected variation

    Current Phase Results (32 weeks of data):

    • Build: 14.8% (12-16% range)
    • Deploy: 13.0% (11-14% range)

    • Project Management: 9.8% (8-11% range)
    • Design: 9.5% (8-10% range)
    • Test: 8.4% (7-9% range)
